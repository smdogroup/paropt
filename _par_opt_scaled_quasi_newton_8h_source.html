7 /*
8 This class is only used when there is a single constraint and objective is
9 linear.
10 
11 In this case:
12  L = f - z*c
13  B ~= Hess = -z*Hess(c)
14 And we define
15  B0 ~= -Hess(c)
16 such that
17  B = z*B0
18 
19 This will give a better approximation to the Hessian of the Lagrangian with B
20 if we use the quasi-Newton update correction.
21 */
